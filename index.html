    Vivek Padmanabha is an Assistant Professor of Department of Civil Engineering (Geotechnical Division) at the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati. He holds a Ph.D. (2018) degree in Civi Engineering from Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore, India. He obtained his Master degree, M.Tech. (2011) in Geotechnical Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur (IITK), India, and a Bachelor degree, B.E. (2008) in Civil Engineering from BMS College of Engineering, Bengaluru, India.

    Before joining Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, he was a Postdoctoral Researcher from April 2019 to Dec 2021 in Department of General Geology and Structural Geology at Albert Ludwig University of Freiburg, Freiburg, Germany. During the above above mentioned period, he also served as an academic scientist at Fraunhofer Institute for High-Speed Dynamics, Freiburg, Germany or popularly known as Ernst Mach Institute (EMI). During Mar 2018 - Mar 2019, he was as a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Indian Institute of Science, on a industry colloborative project with Sarathy Geotech And Engineering Services Pvt. Ltd, Bengaluru, India. Early in his career, he worked as a Research Assistant for short-term at BMS College of Engineering, India (2008) and University of Illinois, Chicago,, USA (2011).

    His research mentors at different stages of his research career include Prof. TG Sitharam, Prof. Frank Schäfer, Prof. Thomas Kenkmann, Prof. Priyanka Ghosh, and Prof. S Raghunath.

    Research Interest : Understanding the High strain-rate behavior of materials, particularly geomaterials like soil, rock and concrete. ; Shock phenomena (attenuation) through granular and porous materials, which includes sand particles, porous rocks, synthetic geotextile materials and others ; Fracture and Fragmentation of rocks subjected to different loading rate from quasi-static events to very high velocity impacts. Adaptive mitigation strategies against extreme events and disasters like blast during war & terrorist attacks, chemical & nuclear explosion, landslides & debris flow, asteroid impacts etc.

    Research Works

      2019-2021 High Strain-rate Effects and Fragmentation in Rocks

    • Geotextile encapsulated sand barrier systems are 'engineered versions of sandbags', which are widely used to date in blast mitigation.
    • A laboratory-scale experimental study is performed to evaluate the performance of the sand barrier systems in mitigating shock waves.
    • The presence of geotextile significantly reduced the stress transfer and gas filtration process through the infill granular materials, thereby increasing the attenuation Response.

      • 2016-2017 Landmine Simulator

    • A landmine simulator was indigenously built to simulate buried blast loading in a laboratory.
    • Different stages of landmine explosion are investigated: Blast interaction phase, Bubble expansion phase and Soil ejecta phase.
    • Blast-sand interaction phenomenon is studied in detail with the help of fast response pressure sensors.
    • Sand ejecta kinematics and impulse transfer to the target structure are analysed using an image processing technique with the aid of high-speed photography.
    • The shock wave emanating from the buried blast is visualised using shadowgraphy technique.
